                               ORDER
------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.  The present complaint has been filed under Section 35 read with
    Section 58(1)(a)(i) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, by the
    Complainant alleging deficiency in service and supply of defective
    goods by the Opposite Parties.

FACTS OF THE COMPLAINT:

2.  The Complainant purchased a "UltraCool Royal 585L Side-by-Side
    Refrigerator" (Model No. UCR-585SBS, Serial No. UC2022-78453) from
    Opposite Party No. 2 (the authorized retailer) on 10th January, 2023,
    for a total consideration of Rs. 1,12,000/- (Rupees One Lakh Twelve
    Thousand Only) inclusive of GST, as evidenced by Tax Invoice No.
    HW/GGN/2023/04521 (Exhibit C-1). The product was manufactured by
    Opposite Party No. 1 and carried a comprehensive warranty of two
    years.

3.  Within three months of purchase, the refrigerator developed the
    following defects:
    (a) The compressor began emitting abnormal noise and the cooling
        efficiency deteriorated significantly.
    (b) The digital display panel malfunctioned, displaying erroneous
        temperature readings.
    (c) The ice-maker unit ceased functioning entirely.

4.  The Complainant lodged a complaint with the authorized service centre
    of Opposite Party No. 1 on 18th April, 2023 (Service Request No.
    SR-2023-GGN-7842). A service engineer visited on 25th April, 2023,
    and replaced the digital display board. However, the compressor
    issue and ice-maker malfunction were not addressed.

5.  Between May 2023 and September 2023, the Complainant made five
    further complaints (Exhibits C-4 to C-8). Each time, temporary
    repairs were attempted, but the fundamental defects persisted. On
    the sixth complaint in October 2023, the service centre informed the
    Complainant that the compressor required complete replacement, which
    would take 45 to 60 days as the part was "not available in India."

6.  The Complainant sent a legal notice dated 15th November, 2023
    (Exhibit C-9) to both Opposite Parties demanding replacement of
    the refrigerator or a full refund along with compensation. Opposite
    Party No. 1 replied on 5th December, 2023 (Exhibit C-10) offering
    only a compressor replacement and denying any obligation to replace
    the entire unit.

ARGUMENTS ON BEHALF OF THE COMPLAINANT:

7.  Learned Counsel for the Complainant submitted that the refrigerator
    purchased is a "defective good" within the meaning of Section 2(10)
    of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, as it suffers from defects in
    manufacturing that render it unfit for the purpose for which it was
    purchased.

8.  It was argued that under Section 2(11) of the Act, "deficiency" means
    any fault, imperfection, shortcoming, or inadequacy in the quality,
    nature, and manner of performance required to be maintained by law.
    The failure to provide effective after-sales service and repair within
    a reasonable time constitutes deficiency in service.

9.  Reliance was placed on the following precedents:
    (a) Maruti Udyog Ltd. v. Susheel Kumar Gabgotra, (2006) 4 CPJ 1
        (NC), wherein it was held that repeated repairs to a new product
        amount to a manufacturing defect and the consumer is entitled to
        replacement.
    (b) General Motors India Pvt. Ltd. v. Ashok Ramnik Lal Shah, 2015
        SCC OnLine NCDRC 1342, wherein the Hon'ble NCDRC directed
        replacement of a vehicle suffering from persistent defects.
    (c) Samsung India Electronics Pvt. Ltd. v. Neerja Monga, 2017 SCC
        OnLine NCDRC 493, wherein compensation was awarded for deficiency
        in after-sales service.

10. The Complainant further submitted that under Section 38 of the
    Consumer Protection Act, 2019, the Commission has wide powers to
    grant relief including replacement, refund, and compensation for
    mental agony and harassment.

ARGUMENTS ON BEHALF OF OPPOSITE PARTIES:

11. Learned Counsel for Opposite Party No. 1 (the manufacturer) contended
    that the product was thoroughly tested before dispatch and bore the
    Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) certification (IS 1476:2018). It was
    argued that the defects arose due to voltage fluctuations at the
    Complainant's residence, which is an external factor beyond the
    manufacturer's control. The warranty terms (Exhibit OP1-3) expressly
    excluded damage caused by voltage fluctuations.

12. Learned Counsel for Opposite Party No. 2 (the retailer) submitted
    that the retailer's role was limited to the sale of the product and
    that liability for defects in the product rests solely with the
    manufacturer. Reference was made to Section 2(34)(ii) of the
    Consumer Protection Act, 2019.

ANALYSIS AND FINDINGS:

13. This Commission has carefully considered the submissions and the
    documentary evidence on record. The contention that the defects were
    caused by voltage fluctuations is not substantiated. The service
    reports (Exhibits C-4 to C-8) prepared by the manufacturer's own
    authorized service engineers do not record any finding of voltage-
    related damage. On the contrary, Service Report dated 25th April,
    2023 (Exhibit C-4) specifically notes "manufacturing defect in
    compressor assembly" as the probable cause.

14. The claim that the product was BIS certified does not absolve the
    manufacturer of liability for individual unit defects. BIS
    certification pertains to type approval and does not guarantee that
    every unit is free from defects. Reference is made to Tata Motors
    Ltd. v. Rajesh Kumar Gupta, 2019 SCC OnLine NCDRC 89.

15. As regards Opposite Party No. 2, while the retailer is primarily an
    intermediary, under Section 2(34) of the Consumer Protection Act,
    2019, a "product seller" includes a person who, in the course of
    business, imports, sells, distributes, or otherwise places the
    product for commercial purposes. Opposite Party No. 2 is therefore
    jointly and severally liable for the defective product sold to the
    Complainant.

16. The fact that six service visits over a period of six months failed
    to rectify the defects establishes beyond doubt that the product
    suffers from an inherent manufacturing defect. The Complainant has
    been deprived of the use of a premium appliance for which he paid
    Rs. 1,12,000/-. This constitutes both supply of defective goods and
    deficiency in service within the meaning of the Consumer Protection
    Act, 2019.

ORDER:

17. In view of the above findings, the complaint is allowed and the
    following directions are issued:

    (a) Opposite Party No. 1 (the manufacturer) is directed to replace
        the defective refrigerator with a brand new unit of the same
        model and specifications, or the nearest equivalent model if the
        original model is discontinued, within 30 days from the date of
        this order. In the event of failure to replace, Opposite Party
        No. 1 shall refund the full purchase price of Rs. 1,12,000/-
        with interest at 9% per annum from the date of purchase.

    (b) Opposite Party No. 1 shall pay compensation of Rs. 2,00,000/-
        (Rupees Two Lakhs Only) to the Complainant towards mental agony,
        harassment, and inconvenience suffered on account of the
        defective product and deficient after-sales service.

    (c) Opposite Party No. 1 shall pay Rs. 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty
        Thousand Only) towards litigation costs.

    (d) Opposite Party No. 2 shall be jointly and severally liable
        for the directions at (a) above in the event of default by
        Opposite Party No. 1.

    (e) The above payments and replacement shall be completed within
        45 days from the date of this order, failing which the amounts
        shall carry additional interest at the rate of 12% per annum.

    (f) The Complainant shall return the defective refrigerator to
        Opposite Party No. 1 at the time of receiving the replacement
        unit, with the collection and logistics to be arranged by
        Opposite Party No. 1 at its own cost.
