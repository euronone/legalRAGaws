------------------------------------------------------------------------
                              JUDGMENT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.  The present suit has been filed by the Plaintiff seeking partition and
    separate possession of his undivided share in the ancestral property
    situated at House No. 14, Chandni Chowk, Old Delhi, Delhi-110006,
    bearing Municipal No. 14/3/2, admeasuring approximately 450 square
    yards (hereinafter referred to as the "Suit Property").

BRIEF FACTS OF THE CASE:

2.  Late Shri Mohan Lal Sharma, the common ancestor of the parties, was
    the original owner of the Suit Property. He passed away intestate on
    12th September, 2001. He was survived by two sons, namely, Shri
    Rajendra Kumar Sharma (the Plaintiff) and Late Shri Suresh Kumar
    Sharma (deceased husband of Defendant No. 1), and one daughter, Smt.
    Kavita Devi (Defendant No. 2).

3.  Late Shri Suresh Kumar Sharma passed away on 3rd January, 2018,
    leaving behind his wife Smt. Anita Sharma (Defendant No. 1) and their
    son Shri Mohit Sharma (Defendant No. 3).

4.  The Plaintiff contends that the Suit Property is ancestral property
    which devolved upon the legal heirs of Late Shri Mohan Lal Sharma by
    survivorship. The Plaintiff claims a one-third share in the Suit
    Property and seeks partition thereof.

5.  The Defendants have been in exclusive possession of the Suit Property
    since the death of Late Shri Suresh Kumar Sharma and have denied the
    Plaintiff any access to or share in the property. Despite repeated
    requests and a legal notice dated 5th June, 2022, the Defendants have
    refused to acknowledge the Plaintiff's rightful share.

ARGUMENTS ON BEHALF OF THE PLAINTIFF:

6.  Learned Senior Counsel for the Plaintiff submitted that under Section
    6 of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956 (as amended by the Hindu
    Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005), the Plaintiff is entitled to a
    share in the coparcenary property by birth. It was argued that the
    property in question is undeniably ancestral property, having been
    acquired by the grandfather of the parties, and has devolved through
    generations without any partition.

7.  Reliance was placed on the landmark judgment of the Hon'ble Supreme
    Court in Prakash & Ors. v. Phulavati & Ors. (2016) 2 SCC 36, wherein
    it was held that the rights under the amended Section 6 are applicable
    to living daughters of living coparceners as on 9th September, 2005.
    Further, reference was made to Vineeta Sharma v. Rakesh Sharma (2020)
    9 SCC 1, wherein the Hon'ble Supreme Court held that the amended
    provision is retroactive in nature and confers rights by birth.

8.  It was further submitted that under Section 19 of the Hindu Succession
    Act, 1956, where two or more heirs succeed together to the property of
    an intestate, they shall take the property per capita and as tenants-
    in-common, and not as joint tenants. Accordingly, the Plaintiff is
    entitled to a defined share.

ARGUMENTS ON BEHALF OF THE DEFENDANTS:

9.  Learned Counsel for the Defendants contended that the Suit Property
    was not ancestral property but was self-acquired by Late Shri Mohan
    Lal Sharma from his own earnings. It was argued that the sale deed
    dated 15th March, 1975 (Exhibit D-1) shows that the property was
    purchased by Shri Mohan Lal Sharma in his individual capacity and
    not from any joint family funds.

10. It was further contended that the Plaintiff had received his share
    during the lifetime of the father by way of a separate residential
    property at Karol Bagh, Delhi, and therefore the suit is barred by
    the principles of estoppel and acquiescence.

11. Reliance was placed on Uttam v. Saubhag Singh (2016) 4 SCC 68,
    wherein the Hon'ble Supreme Court held that a property purchased by
    a person from his self-acquired funds cannot be treated as ancestral
    property merely because it was inherited by his sons.

ISSUES FRAMED:

12. This Court framed the following issues on 10th January, 2024:
    (i)   Whether the Suit Property is ancestral property or self-acquired
          property of Late Shri Mohan Lal Sharma?
    (ii)  Whether the Plaintiff is entitled to partition and separate
          possession of his share?
    (iii) Whether the Plaintiff has relinquished his share by accepting
          an alternative property?
    (iv)  Relief.

ANALYSIS AND FINDINGS:

Issue No. (i):

13. Upon careful examination of the documentary evidence, this Court notes
    that the sale deed (Exhibit D-1) indeed shows the property was
    purchased by Late Shri Mohan Lal Sharma. However, the revenue records
    (Exhibit P-3) and the family settlement deed of 1968 (Exhibit P-4)
    clearly indicate that the consideration for the purchase was paid from
    the joint family business of "M/s Sharma & Sons," a partnership firm
    in which the father and grandfather were partners. The property was
    therefore acquired from joint family nucleus and qualifies as
    coparcenary property. Reference is made to C.N. Arunachala Mudaliar
    v. C.A. Muruganatha Mudaliar, AIR 1954 SC 4.

14. Accordingly, Issue No. (i) is decided in favour of the Plaintiff.

Issue No. (ii):

15. Under Section 8 read with the Schedule to the Hindu Succession Act,
    1956, the Class I heirs of Late Shri Mohan Lal Sharma are entitled
    to succeed to the property in equal shares. The Plaintiff, being a
    Class I heir (son), is entitled to a one-third share in the Suit
    Property, the other two-thirds belonging to Late Shri Suresh Kumar
    Sharma's legal representatives (Defendants 1 and 3) and Smt. Kavita
    Devi (Defendant No. 2) respectively.

Issue No. (iii):

16. The Defendants failed to produce any documentary evidence to
    substantiate the claim that the Karol Bagh property was given to the
    Plaintiff in lieu of his share. No gift deed, family settlement, or
    partition deed was produced. The oral testimony of DW-1 was
    uncorroborated and self-serving. This issue is decided against the
    Defendants.

ORDER:

17. In light of the above findings, this Court passes the following order:

    (a) The suit is hereby decreed in favour of the Plaintiff.

    (b) The Plaintiff is declared entitled to a one-third undivided share
        in the Suit Property.

    (c) A preliminary decree for partition under Order XX Rule 18 of the
        Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, is hereby passed. The Suit Property
        shall be partitioned by metes and bounds. A Local Commissioner is
        appointed to submit a report for the division of the property
        within 60 days from the date of this order.

    (d) In the event the property cannot be conveniently partitioned, the
        property shall be sold and the sale proceeds distributed among the
        parties in proportion to their respective shares.

    (e) The Defendants shall pay costs of the suit to the Plaintiff,
        assessed at Rs. 75,000/- (Rupees Seventy-Five Thousand Only).

    (f) The decree-sheet be drawn up accordingly.
